Sage Culture is pleased to present Flora, a group exhibition featuring works by Daniela Busarello, Mitch Iburg, Michal Fargo, Magdalena Karpinska, Domingos Tótora, Brooke Holm, Valéria Nascimento, John Pule, and Søren Solkaer.

The selected works portray a subtle but profound relation to nature’s rhythm, time passing, and sublime elements of seasons. The eccentricity of Earth’s orbit influences fauna and flora—from bird’s migration to plants to be dormant. Cycles and variations are interpreted in visual abstraction and organic forms.
The nature of beauty is one of the most enduring themes in human history and is—with the nature of art—one of the two fundamental issues in philosophical aesthetics.

Nature as a vehicle for emotions, a blossoming presence since ancient cultures, transmitting a serene interaction between humans and their surroundings.

Through this exhibition and its visual catalog, Sage Culture invites you to discover the respect for the environment behind the inspiration and works of these featured artists.
